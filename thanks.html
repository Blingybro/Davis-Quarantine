What are you thankful for?

There are people risking their lives during a pandemic to keep their community afloat. Schools continue to teach students, even while being physically separated by using technology to teach them from afar. Millions of people are out of jobs because of the virus, and the government has them living on unemployment to still pay the bills and feed their families. So, what am I thankful for? Let's see.

I am thankful for the people working in hospitals, helping out the people who have the virus. It's dangerous work because you can catch it without knowing, and spread it to other people before you even show symptoms. I usually don't like to thank people just for doing their jobs that anyone who is qualified can do, but this time, it's different. So thanks.

I am thankful for the teachers who assign us work. Without them teaching us over the computer, we wouldn't be able to get credit for this school year, and we'd have to repeat. This would lead to chaos because students can only be in high school up to 20 in New Jersey, so in the low chance that someone repeats a grade again, they won't be able to graduate senior year in the Boro.

I am thankful for the parents that provide almost everything I have. We usually don't notice how fortunate we are because of us being in a first world country and not having to worry about meals, but we're lucky to have a roof over our heads and lucky to eat 3 meals a day.
